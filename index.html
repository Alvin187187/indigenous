💬 What is the Community Circle?

A safe, respectful space to share thoughts, discuss traditions, and connect with advocates of indigenous culture.

🤝

Community Code of Conduct

All discussions must remain culturally sensitive and respectful. No mockery, discrimination, or misinformation about indigenous peoples or cultures. Violations will be removed.

Discussion Prompts

🌿 What is one tradition from your province you want to preserve?

🏔️ How can young Filipinos help protect ancestral lands?

🤔 Which tribe's culture surprised you the most?

🇵🇭 How does learning about IP history change your view?

📣 What indigenous community story deserves more respectful coverage?

📚 What should every student learn first about IPRA?

📝 Share Your Thoughts

Responding to: What is one tradition from your province you want to preserve?

Circle Rules

  • Respect living culturesSpeak about communities with care. Avoid stereotypes, jokes, or reducing traditions to costumes or trivia.
  • Verify before postingIf you share a claim about land rights, policy, or tribe history, mention the source or note that it is a question, not a fact.
  • No harmful languageDiscrimination, harassment, and dismissive language toward indigenous peoples, elders, youth, or advocates are not allowed.
  • Protect community privacyDo not post private locations, contact numbers, or sacred practices that should not be publicly documented.
  • Center indigenous voicesWhen possible, amplify direct community statements, local organizations, and research grounded in lived experience.

Before You Post

Ask whether your message helps preserve, understand, protect, or respectfully question. The Circle works best when curiosity is paired with accountability.

Be respectful Credit sources Protect sacred knowledge Report misinformation

🌺 Our Roots - About This Project

A web-based platform built by TIP students to preserve, educate, and advocate for Philippine Indigenous Peoples through smart-mapping and educational gamification.

Our Mission
🛡️

Protect

Raise awareness of the real threats facing indigenous communities - land grabbing, discrimination, and cultural erasure.

📚

Educate

Provide rich, research-based information on each indigenous group through interactive, gamified learning experiences.

🤝

Advocate

Connect users with verified NGOs, current legislation, and actionable ways to support indigenous rights and inclusion.

Our Educational Framework

Katutubo Explorer integrates Educational Gamification (Zeng et al., 2024) with Spatial Learning Theory to create a memorable, low-anxiety learning environment. Our system follows the ARCS model: Attention, Relevance, Confidence, and Satisfaction.

The Smart Mapping feature is grounded in HCI (Human-Computer Interaction) principles, ensuring the platform is accessible, intuitive, and inclusive for all learners.

⚖️ The IPRA - Republic Act 8371 (Simplified)

The Indigenous Peoples Rights Act of 1997 is the landmark law protecting indigenous peoples in the Philippines. It recognizes four major bundles of rights:

Rights to Ancestral Domains and Lands
Right to Self-Governance and Empowerment
Rights to Social Justice and Human Rights
Right to Cultural Integrity and Preservation

Key mechanism: Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC) - no development project may proceed on ancestral land without genuine IP community consent. Enforcement by the NCIP remains a critical challenge.
